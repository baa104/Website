Bad App

BreakTime PDF here.

Like many people, I feel like I spend far too much time on my phone. There have been times where I would lay down in bed to fall asleep only to keep myself up for hours watching YouTube videos. I know that having my phone on so close to my bed is causing me to stay up and that the solution is to simply shut it down and eliminate the distraction, but its just too easy to pick it up and scroll. Its not just YouTube either; I have been late to Zoom meetings in the past because I was too preoccupied scrolling through memes on Instagram. Its way too easy to open a social media app and loose track of time, so I thought it would be interesting if there was an outside force that restricted your access. My app BreakTime does this by giving you a set amount of time each day to go through your socials before locking you out. The app is rather simple. Either through manual user input or automatic calculations based on usage data, BreakTime generates a time limit for each of the user's social media apps. As an example, say I manually input a daily limit of 2 hours in Instagram. Every second I spend in Instagram that day is added up within BreakTime, and when I finally accumulate a grand total of 2 hours spent in Instagram, BreakTime kicks me from the app and sends me back to the home screen whenever I try to reopen it. This is reset after 18 hours, so if I reached my 2 hour limit at 5 PM, I can reopen Instagram at 11 AM. Originally, I intended to make the app limit how much continuous time you could spend in your socials. Since this app is meant to help you not loose track of time and fall down a rabbit hole, this made sense, but there was one main issue; it would be very easy for users to circumvent if they wanted to. If the app tracked continuous time so it could stop you when you reach the limit, you could just close and immediately reopen the app to reset the timer. This would render the app useless to people who likely need it the most. By setting it to be a daily limit, users can no longer cheat the system, and in fact are forced to be more mindful of the limited time they do spend. For my image, I wanted to demonstrate what BreakTime would look like in action. Rather than spend time trying to make a cool icon or fancy UI, I wanted to show readers exactly what to expect if they were to use this app: a no-nonsense reminder that their daily limit has been reached. The home screen was made using a combination of GIMP, and open source alternative to Adobe Photoshop, and Mediamodifier, a (god-awful) free online tool for creating messages from templates. The text and background was made with Canva, another free online tool that lets you create ads and other documents from templates. The main issue I can see this app creating is blocking access to socials in urgent situations. Whether you need to DM a friend/loved one about a family emergency or need to look up a tutorial on how to put out a fire or perform CPR, you are completely out of luck if you have already met your daily limit. Depending on circumstances, you may need to borrow someone else's phone or access your account through a browser, which takes more time and may also stall you with ads.
